Hello! I am a PhD candidate in the Microbiology Doctoral Training Program at the University of Wisconsin-Madison studying engineered microbiomes in Dr. Katherine McMahon's Lab. I am specifically interested in applying modern 'omics techniques to enrichment bioreactor communities to understand the ecology and evolution of engineered microbial systems.

Additionally, I am committed to reproducible and open research practices, and am passionate about helping other researchers with their analytical challenges. I currently co-chair the Computational Biology, Ecology, and Evolution (ComBEE) group at UW-Madison and am a certified Carpentries instructor.

Prior to moving to Madison, I received my B.S. in Biology with a minor in Statistics at the University of Arkansas - Fayetteville. I was an undergraduate research assistant in Dr. Jeffrey Lewis' Lab studying natural variation of stress responses in yeast.

In my free time I enjoy reading science fiction, practicing yoga, and playing the violin. Feel free to contact me about my research, reproducible science, or chat about Igor Stravinsky!
